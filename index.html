Mountaineering

What is Mountaineering?

Mountaineering is an outdoor sport that involves ascending mountains! Other mountain-related activities include:

  • Skiing
  • Rock Climbing
  • Climbing
  • Absailing

Many people develop a passion for mountaineering due to the picturesque views, while others enjoy the peace and serenity. There's a reason to love mountaineering for everyone.

History

The earliest known records of mountaineering originated were not sporting related; To see if heights were haunted, to build altars for worship and to get an overview of the surrounding area for battle reasons. All of these motives brought about the sport of mountaineering. Scientists and Meteorologists would climb peaks to conduct weather readings and collect meteorological data to make predictions about weather patterns and conclude scientific observations.

The first people to climb the highest mountain in the world, Mt. Everest, were Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay. They conquered Mt. Everest on the 29th of May 1953, after a brutal 16 day ascent to the summit of the mountain. This was Norgay's 6th attempt at the climb. Reinhold Messner was the first person to climb all 14 mountains that stand over 8000 meters or 26,347 feet.

Facts and Information

Mountaineering is considered an extreme sport as it involves climbing in harsh conditions and rugged terrain where a lack of knowledge or previous experience could hinder the climb. Hazards such as rocky cliffs, uneven terrain, extreme weather conditions and much more can turn a simple ascent into a fight for survival.

Due to the thinning of the air as the altitude increases, climbers of Mt. Everest carry oxygen tanks to allow them to breathe for long periods of time at the peak. These oxygen tanks weigh at most 8 pounds or 3.6kg on average. The gear list for such an expedition contains equipment such as food, water, clothing, tents, sleeping bags, boots, and climbing equipment, in total weighing anywhere from 35 - 55 pounds or 15 - 25 kg.

Techniques

Hiking

Hiking is probably the most necessary skill to master when it comes to mountaineering. Mountains are climbed by repeatedly stepping one foot in front of the other for hours at a time with not many breaks. This is performed on long steep slopes that make it easy to lose your footing. It is essential you know how to maintain your gear to make the experience as streamlined as possible.

Rock Climbing

Similarly to hiking, rock climbing is another one of the essential skills needed for mountaineering. However it differs in the respect that it is mostly hand work. Knowing where to place your hands, how to balance your center of mass, and strengthening your grip are all a big part of knowing how to rock climb. Rock climbing can be practised indoors at a bouldering gym or can be practised at local cliffs. There are many activities that fall under the umbrella term of rock climbing such as bouldering and lead climbing. Lead climbing involves the use of ropes to secure your harness at varying points along the climbing route.

Absailing

Finally, absailing is the way to descend after a hard climb. It involves anchoring yourself at the top of a cliff face using a tool called belaying anchor, and rapelling down, usually if the slope is too steep to descend normally without protection from a harness. It is essentially controlled falling using friction to slow yourself down as you fall.
